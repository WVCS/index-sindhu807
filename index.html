The Cold War

What is the cold war?

The cold war was a war fought between the Soviet Union and the United States with their respective allies. It was a war based on propaganda, economic, and political fronts as it was rarely fought in person but rather through threats, spying, and manipulation. Each country possessed many weapons, some which could wipe out millions of people. It was a time of fear and anticipation.

Date Description
1945 Yalta Conference: The leaders of the United States, Britain, and the Soviet Union met at the Soviet Black Sea resort of Yalta. They decided to divide Germany into zones of occupation which were controlled by the Allied military forces. They agreed that Germany would have to pay the Soviet Union in order to pay back what they did to that country like the loss of life and property. Stalin, the leader of the Soviet Union decided to also join the war against Japan.
June 1945 Creation of the United Nations: In June of 1954 both the United States and the Soviet Union joined together to create an alliance with another 48 countries and named it the United Nations or UN for short. It was created to protect the members of the UN from aggression. Each member of the UN was able to cast its vote on a various issues. They also created a Security Council that had the real power to investigate and settle disputes. The permanent members of the United Nations were Britain, China, France, the United States, and the Soviet Union. Each country could veto another country’s decision.
1946-1947 Truman Doctrine: President Truman adopted a foreign policy called the “Containment” where he wanted to stop the Soviet influence and the expansion of communism. This was done through making alliances and helping weaker countries resist Soviet advances.He also supported countries that resisted communism by supporting them with money. America spent 400 million dollars in aid to Turkey and Greece.
1948-1949 The Berlin Airlift: The Soviets did not want Germany unified, they wanted it to be broken. The United States and its allies wanted Germany to be unified. Therefore France, Britain, and the United States withdrew their forces from Germany in order for it to unify. However the Soviets held West Berlin hostage and tried to starve the country so that the Allies would surrender West Berlin or give up their idea of unifying Germany. However, American and British officials flew food and resources into West Berlin for 11 months until May 1949 when the Soviets admitted defeat.
1949-1953 Nuclear War: The United States had atomic bombs and the Soviet Union exploded their own in 1949. There was a race for time for who would be able to build a stronger weapon first between the US and the Soviet Union. In 1952 the US tested their first H-bomb (which was stronger than the A-bomb) and in 1953 the Soviet Union exploded their own bomb.

Philosophy

  • United States
    • encouraged Democracy in other countries to help prevent the raise of Communist governments
    • gain access to raw materials and markets to fuel booming industries
    • rebuild European countries to promote stability and create new markets for U.S. goods
    • reunite Germany to stabilize it and increase the security of Europe
  • Soviet Union
    • encouraged Communism in other countries as part of a worldwide workers' revolution
    • rebuild its war-ravaged economy using Eastern Europe's industrial equipment and raw materials
    • control Eastern Europe to protect Soviet borders and balance the US influence in Western Europe
    • keep Germany divided to prevent its waging war again
